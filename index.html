Provenance • Acquisition • Field Investigation

Got something that needs investigating?

Whether it's a family heirloom with a murky past, a recent acquisition you want authenticated, or a lead on something rare—send over what you've got. Photos help, provenance documents help more, but even a hunch is worth discussing.

◈

Provenance

Every artifact has a history—the question is whether anyone bothered to write it down. Cross-referencing auction records, tracking ownership through estate sales, piecing together chains of custody. Most objects have a story worth telling if you know where to look.
⚜

Acquisition

Sometimes you're not trying to understand what you have, you're trying to find what you don't. Locating specific pieces means working through dealer networks and private collections—knowing who specializes in what and who can be trusted. Years in the field build those connections.
✦

Field Investigation

Not everything can be solved from a library desk. Some questions require showing up—municipal archives that don't digitize, private collections that don't advertise, sites that need eyes on the ground. The work involves travel and following leads that might go nowhere. But when they go somewhere, that's when it gets interesting.

Case Studies

A selection of recent investigations—what was found, how the trail unfolded, and what the evidence revealed.

Machiavelli's History of Florence

An 1854 London edition with Victorian binding and an intriguing 1868 inscription. Client wanted the full provenance—publisher, binder, previous owner, the works. Three weeks of digging through records delivered the answers.

The Emerald Ledger

A coded merchant ledger that might have documented 18th century gemstone trade routes. Breaking the cipher was the easy part—proving it wasn't a clever modern forgery required considerably more work.

Steel of the Highlands

A ceremonial blade with no clear origin story. Metallurgy analysis, historical records, and regional smith marks eventually traced it across four months and three countries before the pieces fell into place.
